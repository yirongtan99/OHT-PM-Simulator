## Phase 1: Project Odin & Preliminary FYP Research (Weeks 1 - 6)

### Weeks 1 & 2: Onboarding and Project Odin Initiation
**Focus:** AMHS Familiarization & Project Odin Setup
*   **AMHS Operations:** Familiarized with the day-to-day line support workflow and observed the Preventive Maintenance (PM) processes for the Overhead Hoist Transport (OHT) system.
*   **Project Odin (Wheel Wear & Tear):** Began the continuation of Project Odin. Set up the foundational requirements for measuring OHT wheel wear and tear through sensor testing. Identified the parameters needed for accurate Predictive Maintenance (PdM) data collection.
*   **FYP Scoping:** Began exploring early ideas for an industry-attached Final Year Project (FYP) focused on automating the OHT sensor maintenance process.

### Weeks 3 & 4: Data Collection & Vendor Sourcing
**Focus:** Odin Data Collection & FYP Feasibility Study
*   **Project Odin:** Focused heavily on executing sensor testing and collecting wheel measurement data. Ensured the data collected was reliable for analyzing wheel degradation over time.
*   **FYP Vendor Engagement:** Concurrently began research for the FYP (Semi-Automated PM System). Prepared and presented a slide deck to vendors like OMRON and Universal Robots (UR). 
*   **Feasibility Analysis:** Gathered information on collaborative robot (cobot) capabilities, vision systems, and integration options to evaluate the feasibility of automating the PM connector handling.

### Weeks 5 & 6: Data Sorting & Alternative Sourcing
**Focus:** Odin Data Processing & FYP Pivot
*   **Project Odin:** Continued continuous monitoring and began sorting sensor reading data. Validated the sensor outputs to ensure they accurately reflected wheel conditions.
*   **OMRON Site Visit & Budget Constraints:** Conducted an off-site visit to the OMRON facility for hands-on evaluation of industrial robotics. However, quotes greatly exceeded the project budget.
*   **FYP DIY Alternatives:** Pivoted research towards more cost-effective DIY solutions, such as proposing a centralized single magnetic plug to bypass the need for expensive vision systems, alongside researching the necessary electrical circuit redesigns (e.g., relay systems).

## Phase 2: Transition to FYP Prototype Development (Weeks 7 - 8)

### Weeks 7 & 8: Software Development & AI Training
**Focus:** Shifting priority to the FYP (PM Workflow Simulator)
*   **Software Development (C#):** Shifted main focus to developing the offline Windows-based simulator in C# (WinForms). Reorganized the simulator codebase for better maintainability and refined the user interface to accommodate run logging and screenshot generation.
*   **AI Model Training:** Reviewed and sorted over 400 sensor reading images into "Obstacle Detected" and "Clear" categories. Used this dataset to train an Artificial Intelligence (AI) model to automate the verification of sensor functionality.
*   **Testing & Refinement:** Currently testing the AI model against sensor boundary conditions. Acknowledged the challenge of not being able to test with live sensors on the OHT, necessitating the use of simulated/offline environments for the time being.
